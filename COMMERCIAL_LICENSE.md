## Why FSL-1.1?

Sicario previously used the Business Source License 1.1 (BSL 1.1). We migrated to FSL-1.1 for three reasons:

1. **Simpler non-compete language.** FSL-1.1's non-compete clause is narrower and clearer: it prohibits only running Sicario as a competing hosted scanning service. It does not restrict internal use, CI/CD pipelines, open-source projects, or non-profits.

2. **Automatic Apache 2.0 conversion.** Each version of Sicario automatically becomes Apache 2.0 two years after its first public release — not on a fixed calendar date. This means the community always knows exactly when any given version goes fully open source.

3. **Fair Source alignment.** FSL-1.1 is part of the [Fair Source](https://fair.io) movement, which provides a recognized, community-vetted framework for sustainable open development. Using FSL-1.1 signals that Sicario is committed to eventual full open-source release while protecting the ability to fund ongoing development.
